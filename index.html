Research Interests

My research aims to develop control strategies and algorithms that balance performance, robustness, and computational efficiency for complex dynamical systems. My research integrates rigorous mathematical modeling with data-driven intelligence, aiming to create robust and interpretable solutions for real-world engineering challenges.

Mathematical Systems Theory

Developing models and control strategies for dynamical systems based on mathematical and physical principles, including estimation theory, optimal control, and observer design:

  • State and disturbance estimation – enhancing control accuracy through observers and filtering.

  • Optimization-based control – deriving optimal strategies for constrained systems.

  • Stochastic and robust control – handling uncertainties in practical applications.

Data-Driven Dynamical Systems

Leveraging statistical learning, time-series analysis, and machine learning to identify, model, and control dynamical systems directly from data:

  • Data-driven modeling – building models from observed data without relying solely on physical equations (e.g., SINDy, Neural Networks, etc).

  • Data-driven control – optimizing system performance directly from system data (e.g., Neural Networks, Reinforcement Learning, etc).

Applications

Robotics and autonomous systems, aerospace and UAVs, manufacturing process control, biological and environmental systems, and renewable energy systems.
